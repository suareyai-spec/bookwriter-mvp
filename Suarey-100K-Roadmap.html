Suarey — 100K in 90 Days Roadmap

The Math

Current: ~5,000 followers
Target: 100,000 followers
Timeline: 90 days
Platform: Instagram (primary), TikTok (secondary accelerant)

How it actually works:

  • Days 1-30: Grinding. Building the content machine. 5K → 8-12K.

  • Days 30-60: One or two videos cross 500K+ views. 12K → 25-40K.

  • Days 60-90: Compounding. Algorithm trusts you. Multiple videos hitting. 40K → 100K.


This isn't guaranteed. But this is how every account that's done it in 90 days describes the pattern.

Phase 1: The Machine (Days 1-30)

Volume + Testing

Post 2 Reels per day. Not 1. Two. Here's why:

  • At 1/day, you get 30 shots at a breakout video in the first month

  • At 2/day, you get 60 shots

  • The algorithm needs data to figure out who to show you to. More content = more data = faster optimization


Morning post: Food content (your highest-performing category)
Evening post: Rotate between music, Celadon, travel, lifestyle

The 10 Food Formats to Test

Film all of these in week 1-2. See which ones pop. Then double down on what works.

1. "You're walking past this place" — Walk in off the street, show one hero dish, walk out. 12 seconds.

2. "$5 vs $50 [same dish]" — Side by side comparison of a cheap version vs expensive version in Miami. Extremely shareable.

3. "I asked the chef to make whatever they want" — No menu ordering. Let the chef decide. Film what arrives. People love the surprise element.

4. "Rating Miami's most hyped restaurants" — Go to a place everyone talks about. Be honest. Controversial opinions get shares.

5. "What $20 gets you in [neighborhood]" — Budget challenge in Little Havana, Wynwood, Brickell, etc. Each neighborhood is a separate video. Series content keeps people coming back.

6. "My Cuban mom rates [trending food]" — If your mom is willing, this is GOLD. The Cuban mom reaction to sushi burritos, birria tacos, etc. Cultural clash = engagement.

7. "The dish that put [restaurant] on the map" — One restaurant, one legendary dish, the story behind it. 20-30 seconds.

8. "I ate at every restaurant on one block" — Pick a block in Calle Ocho or Wynwood. Hit every spot. Rank them at the end.

9. "Things you didn't know about Miami food" — Quick facts. "The colada window was invented in Hialeah." Educational + local pride = shares from Miami accounts.

10. "Late night Miami eats (after midnight)" — Most food content is daytime. The late-night angle is underserved and hits a different audience.

Production Rules for Speed

  • Film 3-5 Reels in one outing. Don't go out for one video.

  • Edit on your phone. CapCut is fine. Keep it under 20 minutes of editing per video.

  • Film horizontally AND vertically so you have options.

  • Save all b-roll. A 30-second clip you didn't use today might be the perfect transition next week.

  • Batch your caption writing. Write all captions at the end of the day, not per post.


Engagement Work (Non-Negotiable)

30 minutes per day. Split it:

  • 10 min: Reply to EVERY comment on your posts within the first hour of posting. The algorithm boosts posts with early engagement.

  • 10 min: Comment on 10 Miami food/lifestyle accounts (50K+ followers). Real comments, not "fire" or emoji. Say something specific about the content.

  • 10 min: DM 5 people per day — restaurant owners, other creators, Miami New Times writers. Not pitching. Just connecting. "loved your piece on X" or "that dish looks insane, is it still on the menu?"


Week 1-2 Specific:


  • Post 14 Reels (testing all formats)

  • Identify your top 3 performing formats (look at views, not likes)

  • Set up TikTok if not already active. Cross-post everything.


Week 3-4 Specific:


  • Double down on top 3 formats

  • Start a series (give it a name — something simple like "Miami $20 Challenge" or "Walking Into...")

  • Series create return viewers. Return viewers = followers.

  • Reach out to 3 restaurant owners for collab opportunities


End of Phase 1 target: 8,000-12,000 followers

Phase 2: The Breakout (Days 30-60)

What Changes

By now you know what works. You've posted 60+ Reels. You have data. Now you optimize ruthlessly.

The Breakout Formula

Videos that cross 500K+ views almost always have:
1. A hook in the first 0.5 seconds — text on screen or a visual that stops the scroll
2. Tension or surprise — something unexpected happens
3. Local pride or debate — "Best Cuban sandwich in Miami" makes every Cuban person share it to argue
4. Rewatchability — fast enough that people watch it twice

Tactical Moves

Collab week (Day 30-37): Film with 3-4 other Miami creators. Not formal collabs with matching outfits. Just eating together, filming together. Each of you posts and tags. Cross-pollination is the fastest organic growth hack that exists.

The controversial take (use once): Post a genuine hot take about Miami food. "Versailles is overrated." "The best Cuban coffee isn't in Little Havana." These get shared by people agreeing AND disagreeing. Engagement doesn't care about sentiment.

Pitch Miami New Times for a video series. You already write for them. Propose a Reels series under their brand — "David Eats Miami" or whatever. If they post it to their account (100K+ followers) and tag you, that's a massive boost.

Start featuring your other pillars subtly:

  • Wear Celadon pieces in every food video. Don't mention them.

  • Have your music playing in the background of a food Reel. Don't mention it.

  • When people ask about the ring or the song — answer in comments. This is how you build the multi-pillar brand without diluting the food growth.


TikTok Acceleration

By day 30, you should have 30+ TikToks posted. Look at which ones TikTok pushes vs Instagram. Often different content works on each platform.

TikTok-specific move: Reply to comments with video. Someone comments "where is this?" — you make a new video responding. TikTok's algorithm heavily promotes reply videos.

End of Phase 2 target: 25,000-40,000 followers (across platforms)

Phase 3: The Compound (Days 60-90)

What's Different Now

At 25-40K, the algorithm treats you differently:

  • Your Reels get shown to a wider initial audience

  • You start appearing in Explore/For You more consistently

  • Brands start reaching out (don't accept anything yet — wait until 100K for better rates)

  • Other creators want to collab with YOU now


Daily Output

Still 2 Reels/day. But now:

  • 1 food Reel (your bread and butter)

  • 1 "world of Suarey" Reel (music, Celadon, travel, crossover content)


You've earned the right to diversify. Your food audience is locked in. Now show them the rest of your life.

The 100K Push Tactics

Go Live once a week. Instagram pushes Live content heavily to your existing followers and sends notifications. Go live from a restaurant kitchen, from a DJ set, from a jewelry workshop. Keep it 15-20 min.

Create a shareable series format. Something people tag friends in. "Miami Food Court" where you rank 3 spots in 30 seconds. "Would You Eat This?" showing weird Miami fusion dishes.

User-generated content: Repost stories where people visit restaurants you recommended. This builds community and shows social proof. "You guys actually went" is powerful.

Local press: By 40K+ followers covering Miami food, you ARE local press. Restaurants will invite you to openings. New spots will send free meals. Say yes to everything — each one is content.

The Music Breakout (Day 75-90)

Now that you have the audience, drop a single with a food-connected Reel. DJ set at a restaurant. A track that samples kitchen sounds. Something that bridges your food audience into your music. Don't just say "new track out" — create a MOMENT that your food followers would share even if they didn't know you made music.

End of Phase 3 target: 80,000-100,000 followers

The Non-Negotiable Daily Routine

Total time commitment: 3-4 hours/day

On shoot days (2-3x/week), block 3-4 hours and film 6-8 Reels at once. Then edit and schedule throughout the week.

What Makes This Work or Fail

It works if:


  • You actually post 2x/day for 90 days straight (180 Reels)

  • You're honest and opinionated in your takes (not trying to please everyone)

  • You study your analytics weekly and kill formats that don't work

  • You engage with your audience like they're friends, not fans

  • You shoot in Miami's most recognizable spots (algorithm favors location-tagged content in popular areas)


It fails if:


  • You skip days. Consistency is 80% of this game.

  • You try to make every video perfect. Speed beats polish at this stage.

  • You avoid controversial takes to keep everyone happy. Boring = invisible.

  • You try to push music/jewelry too early before the food audience is built.

  • You compare yourself to accounts that took 3 years to get where they are. They didn't have this strategy.


The Honest Assessment

Is 100K in 90 days realistic? It's in the top 5% of outcomes. Most accounts doing this strategy would hit 30-50K in 90 days, which is still incredible growth from 5K.

What gets you to 100K: One video hitting 5M+ views. That's largely luck + preparation. You can't manufacture virality, but you can maximize your shots on goal. At 180 Reels in 90 days, your odds are significantly better than someone posting 3x/week.

The guaranteed outcome: Even if you land at 40K instead of 100K, you'll have:

  • 180 pieces of content (a library that keeps working for months)

  • A proven format you know works

  • Restaurant relationships across Miami

  • A feed that impresses anyone who lands on it

  • The foundation to hit 100K by month 5-6


That's worth the 90 days regardless of the exact follower count.
