# Suarey — Social Brand Strategy 2026

## The Brand

**Suarey** isn't a music project or a jewelry line or a food account. It's a lifestyle — a Cuban American guy in Miami who DJs, designs emerald jewelry, eats everywhere, and travels constantly. The audience doesn't follow one thing. They follow the person.

The mistake most multi-hyphenate creators make: they try to separate everything into different accounts. Don't. One brand. One feed. The variety IS the brand.

**Positioning:** "Miami creative who lives well" — not influencer, not DJ, not jeweler. A taste-maker. Someone whose life people want a window into.

---

## The Pillars

### 1. Music — "Suarey Sound"
- **What:** DJ sets, studio sessions, new releases, event recaps, behind-the-scenes production
- **Content ratio:** 25% of posts
- **Formats that work:**
  - 15-30s Reels of live DJ moments (the crowd, the drop, the energy)
  - Studio session snippets — screen recording of Ableton/FL with the track playing. Show the process, not just the result
  - "Track of the week" stories — share what you're listening to, build taste-maker credibility
  - Longer YouTube/TikTok of full sets at venues (10-30 min)
- **Release strategy:** Single every 6 weeks. Each single gets a 2-week content cycle:
  - Week -1: Teaser (studio clip, 15s of the hook)
  - Release day: Full announcement, Spotify link, visual
  - Week +1: Behind the scenes, "how I made this," play it live at a gig
- **Growth hack:** Collab with other Miami DJs on tracks. Cross-pollinate audiences. Miami electronic scene is tight — use it.
- **Key metric to chase:** Spotify monthly listeners (49 → 500 by end of 2026)

### 2. Casa de Celadon — "The Emerald Life"
- **What:** Colombian emerald jewelry and clothing with real emeralds
- **Content ratio:** 20% of posts
- **Formats that work:**
  - Close-up product shots with natural light (emeralds photograph beautifully)
  - "The making of" — show the stones, the settings, the craftsmanship. Luxury buyers want to see the process
  - Wearing the pieces casually — at dinner, DJing, traveling. NOT catalog-style posed shots. Lifestyle integration
  - Story behind each stone: "This emerald came from [mine] in Colombia." Heritage sells
  - GIA study content: "Learning gemology" — positions you as an expert, not just a seller
- **Don't do yet:** Don't hard-sell on the feed. Build desire first. The first 6 months should be "look at this beautiful thing" not "buy this." When people DM asking where to get it, you've won.
- **Soft launch Q4 2026:** Build the waitlist and desire now. Drop pieces as limited runs. Scarcity + quality = luxury.
- **Cuban heritage angle:** Colombian emeralds + Cuban American designer = a story nobody else has. Lean into it hard.

### 3. Food — "Suarey Eats"
- **What:** Restaurant reviews, food experiences, cooking occasionally, Miami food scene coverage
- **Content ratio:** 35% of posts (this is your top performer — ride it)
- **Formats that work:**
  - First-bite Reels (the money shot — close-up of the food, then your reaction)
  - "Hidden gems" series — places tourists don't know about. This is the Miami New Times angle
  - Quick ranking lists: "Top 5 Cuban sandwiches in Miami" — extremely shareable
  - Kitchen content if you cook at all — even simple stuff. "My mom's recipe for X" hits different when you're Cuban
  - Collab with restaurant owners/chefs — they share your content to their audience
- **Miami New Times tie-in:** Every piece you do for them should have a social companion. Article drops, you post the Reel version. Double the reach.
- **Why this works:** Food is the easiest content to go viral. Low barrier to engagement (everyone eats). Use it as the top of funnel that brings people into the Suarey world, where they discover the music and jewelry.

### 4. Travel — "Always Moving"
- **What:** Travel content as a natural byproduct of your life, not a dedicated travel account
- **Content ratio:** 20% of posts
- **Formats that work:**
  - Arrival Reels: quick montage of the flight, landing, first impression
  - "What I ate in [City]" — combines travel + food
  - DJ gigs in other cities — music + travel
  - Jewelry shopping abroad (Colombia, gem shows) — travel + Celadon
  - "Miami vs [City]" comparisons — always ties back home
- **Rule:** Every travel post should connect to at least one other pillar. Travel alone is generic. Travel + food, travel + music, travel + emeralds = uniquely Suarey.

## Platform Strategy

### Instagram (@suareytequiero) — PRIMARY
- **Current:** 4,766 followers, 53.6K views/30 days
- **Target:** 15,000 followers by end of 2026
- **Post frequency:** 4-5 Reels/week, daily stories
- **Feed aesthetic:** Warm tones, Miami light, emerald green accents (naturally from jewelry and your brand color). Not overly curated — lifestyle, not catalog
- **Stories strategy:** This is where personality lives. Polls, Q&As, day-in-the-life, behind the scenes. Stories build the relationship; Reels build the reach.
- **Reels strategy:**
  - Hook in first 1 second (text overlay or visual surprise)
  - 15-30 seconds max for food/lifestyle
  - 30-60 seconds for music/Celadon storytelling
  - Always use trending audio when it fits naturally. Don't force it.
  - Hashtags: 5-8 targeted, not 30 generic ones. #MiamiFood #MiamiDJ #ColombianEmeralds #MiamiLife #NewPop

### TikTok — GROWTH ENGINE
- **Current:** Not sure if active (need to set up or audit)
- **Why:** Fastest organic growth for food and lifestyle content. The algorithm doesn't care about follower count — good content gets pushed
- **Post frequency:** 5-7/week (can be repurposed from Instagram Reels)
- **What hits on TikTok:**
  - Food reviews with honest reactions
  - "Day in my life" (DJ + food + jewelry in one video)
  - Hot takes about Miami
  - Music production clips (TikTok loves behind-the-scenes creative process)
- **Don't do:** Don't make TikTok-specific content at first. Repurpose your best Reels. When something pops, make more of that.

### YouTube — LONG FORM (Phase 2, Q3 2026)
- **Format:** "Suarey" — a lifestyle vlog/docuseries
- **Episodes:** 10-20 min, bi-weekly
  - "I found the perfect emerald in Bogota"
  - "Playing my first big venue in Miami"
  - "Rating every Cuban restaurant on Calle Ocho"
  - "Building a luxury jewelry brand from scratch"
- **Why wait until Q3:** Build the Instagram/TikTok audience first so YouTube launches to an existing audience, not zero

### Spotify
- **Current:** 49 monthly listeners, "Suarey," New Pop/electronic
- **Strategy:** Every social post about music should have a Spotify link. Period.
- **Playlist pitching:** Submit every single to Spotify editorial playlists 2 weeks before release via Spotify for Artists
- **Create your own playlist:** "Suarey's Rotation" — 50 tracks mixing your music with similar artists. Share it in stories. People follow the playlist, then they find your tracks.

## Content Calendar Framework

**Monday:** Music Monday — studio clip, new track teaser, or DJ set recap
**Tuesday:** Food content — restaurant review or recipe
**Wednesday:** Celadon/lifestyle — jewelry, outfit, behind the scenes
**Thursday:** Food or travel — Miami New Times content, travel eats
**Friday:** Music — release day (if applicable), weekend set promo, or playlist share
**Saturday:** Stories only — real-time content from wherever you are (gig, dinner, travel)
**Sunday:** Recap/personal — reflection post, week highlights, or something vulnerable/real

This is a framework, not a prison. Swap days as needed. The point is consistent output across all pillars.

## Growth Tactics (0 Budget)

1. **Collab Reels with Miami food accounts** — there are dozens with 50K-500K followers. Propose a "hidden gem" series together. They get content, you get their audience.

2. **DJ at restaurants/bars that let you film** — combines music + venue promotion. The venue shares your content. Win-win.

3. **Tag Miami New Times in everything** — their account has massive reach. When they reshare you, that's thousands of eyeballs.

4. **Comment strategy:** Spend 15 min/day leaving genuine comments on Miami food, music, and lifestyle accounts. Not "nice pic" — actual thoughts. This puts your name in front of their audience.

5. **Emerald giveaway (Q4):** When Casa de Celadon launches, give away a small piece. "Follow + share to enter." Giveaways still work for jewelry because the perceived value is high.

6. **Cross-promote your pillars:** Every music fan should know about the food content. Every food follower should know you DJ. Every jewelry buyer should see the lifestyle. The algorithm rewards accounts that keep people on the platform — variety helps, not hurts.

---

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't create separate accounts** for music, food, jewelry. You'll split your energy and audience. One brand.
- **Don't post without a hook.** If the first frame doesn't stop the scroll, delete it and reshoot.
- **Don't over-edit.** Your audience is Miami — they want real, not polished. iPhone quality is fine. Authenticity > production value for lifestyle content.
- **Don't sell jewelry on the feed before Q4.** Build desire. Let people see you wearing it. Let them ask. Then open the store.
- **Don't ignore DMs.** Every DM is a potential fan, customer, or collaborator. Reply to all of them for the first year.
- **Don't chase viral.** Chase consistent. 4-5 posts a week for 6 months beats one viral video that you can't replicate.

In 12 months, "Suarey" should mean something in Miami. Not famous — known. The guy who plays great music, wears beautiful emeralds, knows every restaurant, and is always somewhere interesting.
