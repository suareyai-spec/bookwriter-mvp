# KOSIQ Clinical Requirements — From Dr. J.D. Suarez (CMO)

## High-Risk Patient Identification Programs

These are specific patient populations that KOSIQ must identify and flag for action:

### 1. SMI (Serious Mental Illness)
- Identify patients with metabolic disorders AND serious mental health issues
- Flag for enrollment in SMI program
- Cross-reference diagnosis codes: metabolic (E08-E13, E65-E88) + mental health (F20-F48)

### 2. HIV Program Matching
- Identify HIV-positive patients (B20-B24) NOT enrolled in Clear Choice Alliance or similar programs
- Flag as opportunity for program enrollment

### 3. End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD)
- Identify ESRD patients (N18.6) who may benefit from specialized insurance programs
- Flag patients not receiving appropriate ESRD services

### 4. Stage 3b/4 Renal Disease — Early Intervention
- Identify patients with CKD Stage 3b (N18.31) or Stage 4 (N18.4)
- Flag for intervention program enrollment before progression to ESRD

### 5. Uncontrolled Diabetes (HbA1c > 9)
- Identify patients with hemoglobin A1c greater than 9
- Flag for aggressive therapy intervention
- Track lab results / diagnosis codes (E11.65, Z79.4)

### 6. Asthma/COPD Frequent Hospitalizers
- Identify asthma (J44-J45) and COPD patients with frequent hospital admissions
- Evaluate current medications for appropriateness
- Flag for medication review and care plan adjustment

### 7. Non-Compliant / Unengaged Patients
- KEY STAT: 40% of ER patients have never seen their PCP
- Flag patients going to ER/admitted who have never visited their assigned clinic
- Flag NEW members immediately upon assignment for outreach
- Priority: get them engaged with PCP before they become ER utilizers

### 8. Age-Provider Mismatch
- Detect children assigned to geriatric/adult doctors
- Detect adults assigned to pediatricians
- Flag for immediate reassignment to appropriate provider
- This is a known payer auto-assignment error

## Key Insight
These programs represent direct revenue/savings opportunities for managed care:
- Proper program enrollment = better reimbursement
- Early intervention = reduced hospitalizations
- PCP engagement = reduced ER costs
- Correct provider assignment = better outcomes + compliance
